The maker of an era

Shivaji Bhonsale I was an Indian warrior-king and a member of the Bhonsle Maratha clan

Here's a time line of Chattarpati Shivaji Maharaj :

  • 1627- Born in Shivneri, Pune
  • Shivaji's paternal grandfather Maloji (1552–1597) was an influential general of Ahmadnagar Sultanate
  • Shivaji's father Shahaji Bhonsle was a Maratha general who served the Deccan Sultanates
  • Shivaji was named after a local deity, the god Shiva.
  • Shivaji was devoted to his mother Jijabai, who was deeply religious. His studies of the Hindu epics, the Ramayana and the Mahabharata, also influenced his lifelong defence of Hindu values.
  • Many of Shivaji's comrades, and later a number of his soldiers, came from the Maval region, including Yesaji Kank, Suryaji Kakade, Baji Pasalkar, Baji Prabhu Deshpande and Tanaji Malusare.
  • Shivaji traveled the hills and forests of the Sahyadri range with his Maval friends, gaining skills and familiarity with the land that would prove useful in his military career.
  • November 1659: Shivaji Killed Afzal Khan, general of Bijapur.
  • 1659: Siege of Panhala. Shivaji wins.
  • Conflict with the Mughals: when two of Shivaji's officers raided the Mughal territory near Ahmednagar
  • Attacks on Shaista Khan and Surat. Shivaji launched a surprised attack on Shaista Khan in Pune. Shivaji wins.
  • Treaty of Purandar (1665): signed between Shivaji and Jai Singh on 11 June 1665, Shivaji agreed to give up 23 of his forts, keeping 12 for himself, and pay compensation of 400,000 gold hun to the Mughals.
  • Arrest in Agra and escape: In 1666 Aurangzeb summoned Shivaji to Agra along with his nine-year-old son Sambhaji and make him house arrest. SHivaji somehow managed to escape from that fort by smuggling himself along with his son in the basket.
  • Reconquest: Shivaji launched an offensive against the Mughals and recovered a major portion of the territories surrendered to them in a span of four months.
  • In late March 1680, Shivaji fell ill with fever dying around 3–5 April 1680 at the age of 52, on the eve of Hanuman Jayanti.
  • He also took the title of Haindava Dharmodhhaarak (protector of the Hindu faith).

    • Shivaji was admired for his heroic exploits and clever stratagems in the contemporary accounts of English, French, Dutch, Portuguese and Italian writers. Contemporary English writers compared him with Alexander, Hannibal and Julius Caesar. Muslim writers of the day generally described him as a plunderer and marauder. Shivaji didn't loot, he plundered Mughals.
